about her

Jyotsna Penumaka is a Computer Science Master's student at Columbia University, driven by a passion for technology's role in global security. She holds a strong background in computer science and software development, with proficiency in Python, Rust, C, C++, Java, and more. Jyotsna earned her Bachelor's in Computer Engineering from Boston University, where she received the BU Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program Award. As a software engineer at Red Hat, she integrated Rekor with Sigstore-rs, enhancing software supply chain security. She also led a cross-company team in developing 'sget-rs,' collaborating closely with Google's Open Source Security team. Her academic journey includes roles as a Graduate Research Assistant and Research Assistant, with diverse projects spanning Natural Language Processing, Computer Networks, Security, and Ethical Hacking. Jyotsna's recent experience at HP involved identifying vulnerabilities in an HP application through extensive static and dynamic analysis. She received the prestigious HP Intern-Stellar award for Technical Impact, distinguishing her as one of the top interns nationally. Beyond tech, Jyotsna enjoys binge-watching and running. She brings valuable skills and experience to any tech team.
